POPIA COMPLIANCE — SECURITY AWARENESS OBLIGATIONS
Under POPIA, RetailCo SA has obligations to train all staff who process personal information. Section 19 requires appropriate organisational measures — security awareness training is a direct obligation. Section 55 places responsibility on the Information Officer to ensure staff understand their data protection duties.

POPIA SECTION COMPLIANCE MAPPING

Section 19
Security Safeguards
Responsible parties must implement appropriate, reasonable technical and organisational measures to prevent loss, damage or unauthorised access to personal information. Security awareness training is a direct organisational measure under this section.
IN PROGRESS
Section 20
Processing by Operators
Operators processing personal information on behalf of RetailCo must be trained on their obligations. Staff acting as operators for customer PII must complete Module T-003 POPIA & Data Protection training.
IN PROGRESS
Section 22
Notification of Security Compromises
All staff must understand their obligation to report suspected data breaches immediately. The phishing simulation programme directly trains this behaviour — report rate tracks whether employees are reporting suspicious emails rather than ignoring them.
IMPROVING
Section 55
Information Officer Duties
The Information Officer is responsible for ensuring POPIA compliance across the organisation. Running a documented, measured security awareness programme with tracked completion rates is direct evidence of the Information Officer fulfilling this duty.
